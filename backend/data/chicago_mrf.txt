CHICAGO MRF SPECIFICATIONS
Facility: City of Chicago Blue Cart Program — Waste Management MRF, Elmhurst IL
City: Chicago, Illinois
Program Type: Single-stream curbside recycling (blue cart)
Last Updated: 2024

--- PLASTICS ---
Accepted resin codes: #1 (PETE/PET) and #2 (HDPE) only.
Resin codes #3–#7 are NOT accepted.
Black plastic is NOT accepted regardless of resin code. NIR optical sorting cannot detect carbon-black pigmented plastic. Black takeout containers, black deli trays, black lids — trash only.
Accepted bottles and jugs: water bottles, soda bottles, juice bottles, milk jugs, detergent jugs, shampoo bottles. Must be empty and rinsed.
Accepted rigid containers (#1 and #2 only): yogurt tubs, butter tubs, deli containers — clear or non-black colors only.
Plastic bags, film, and flexible plastic are NOT accepted. Place all items loose in the cart. Return plastic bags to grocery store collection bins.
Plastic clamshell containers are NOT accepted — too flat for optical sorter detection.
Plastic cups and cutlery are NOT accepted.
Styrofoam (EPS) is NOT accepted: foam cups, foam plates, foam packaging, foam trays.
Hard plastic items (toys, buckets, laundry baskets, storage bins) are NOT accepted — only bottles, jugs, and tubs.

--- PAPER AND CARDBOARD ---
Corrugated cardboard accepted. Must be broken down flat and dry. Wet cardboard is rejected — keep inside until collection day.
Accepted: newspaper, office paper, magazines, catalogs, junk mail, envelopes.
Paperboard accepted: cereal boxes, tissue boxes, cracker boxes, shoe boxes. Remove and trash plastic liners.
Milk cartons, juice cartons, and Tetra Pak cartons accepted.
Paper bags accepted.
Pizza boxes accepted with minor grease. Heavily saturated boxes should be composted or trashed.
Shredded paper must be sealed in a paper bag or envelope before placing in cart. Do not place loose.
Paper coffee cups with plastic lining are NOT accepted.
Waxed cardboard is NOT accepted.
Thermal paper receipts are NOT accepted.

--- GLASS ---
Glass is NOT accepted in the blue cart.
Chicago's MRF is not equipped to process glass in the single-stream line. Glass breaks during collection, creates sharp hazards, and contaminates paper bales.
Glass drop-off: 17 city locations including Midway Airport, O'Hare Airport, and various ward service centers.
Drop-off accepts: clear, brown, and green glass bottles and jars.
NEVER put glass in the blue cart.

--- METALS ---
Aluminum cans accepted. Empty and rinse.
Steel and tin food cans accepted. Empty and rinse.
Empty aerosol cans accepted.
Aluminum foil and foil trays accepted if rinsed or wiped clean. Crumple into a ball at least 2 inches in diameter.
Metal lids and caps: place inside a steel can and crimp or tape the top closed.
Wire hangers are NOT accepted. Return to dry cleaners or drop off at participating stores.
Scrap metal, appliances, and large metal items are NOT accepted.

--- TANGLERS ---
NEVER put in blue cart:
Plastic bags and plastic film.
Garden hoses of any material or length.
Extension cords, electrical cords, phone chargers, USB cables.
Christmas lights and decorative lighting.
Rope, twine, string, ribbon, yarn.
Clothing, textiles, and fabric.
Metal wire and wire hangers.
Chains of any length.
Rubber tubing.

--- CHICAGO-SPECIFIC RULES ---
Blue cart is for recycling only — NEVER for trash. Contaminated loads are rejected and sent to landfill. Carts can be tagged under Chicago's cart monitoring program.
Chicago does NOT have a citywide curbside composting program as of 2024. Food waste goes in trash unless a community composting drop-off is available nearby.
Wet cardboard from snow or rain is rejected. Keep boxes dry and inside until collection day.
Hard plastics (storage bins, buckets, lawn chairs, toys) are NOT accepted — blue cart accepts bottles, jugs, and small rigid tubs only.

--- SPECIAL DISPOSAL ---
Electronics: City of Chicago e-waste drop-off events and Best Buy stores.
Batteries: Call2Recycle drop-off at Home Depot, Staples, and Best Buy. Never trash — lithium-ion batteries cause fires.
Motor oil: AutoZone, O'Reilly, Advance Auto Parts.
Medications: Chicago Police District station drop boxes.
Hazardous household waste: Chicago HHW facilities.
Textiles: USAgain collection bins at retail stores and participating drop-off locations.

--- COMMON MISTAKES ---
Bagging recyclables in a plastic bag: contaminates the load and wraps around equipment. Place items LOOSE in the cart.
Putting glass in the blue cart: glass breaks and contaminates paper bales. Use city glass drop-off sites.
Recycling black plastic containers: NIR cannot detect carbon-black plastic. Trash only.
Recycling wet cardboard: wet paper has no fiber value and contaminates dry bales. Keep cardboard dry.
Recycling plastic clamshells: Chicago accepts bottles, jugs, and tubs only — not flat clamshells.
